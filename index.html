‚öóÔ∏è ENZYMES

Enzyme: A biological catalyst / protein that speeds up the rate of a reaction
Substrate: The reactant in an enzyme-catalysed reaction / molecule that binds to the active site
Active site: Region with complementary shape to substrate
Stereospecificity: Only ONE enantiomer fits

Models

Lock & Key = Fixed shape
Induced Fit = Flexible shape

Inhibition

Competitive: Similar shape to substrate ‚Üí competes for active site
Drug design: Many drugs work by enzyme inhibition
üéØ Common Exam Question:

üß¨ DNA STRUCTURE

Nucleotide Components

  1. Phosphate group (dihydrogen phosphate)
  2. Pentose sugar (2-deoxyribose)
  3. Organic base:
    • Purines: A, G (double ring)
    • Pyrimidines: C, T (single ring)
Base Pairing Rules:
A-T = 2 H-bonds
C-G = 3 H-bonds
"AT 2gether, CG has 3"
‚ö†Ô∏è Strands separate WITHOUT breaking covalent bonds!
